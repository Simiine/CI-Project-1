Cosmetic Product Types

Identifying the category of a product you are going to make is a vital step in the skincare making process. It is the first step to makeing a safe and stable product. Every category will have its own set of guidelines and methods, which is all based on the solubility and type of ingredients used.

There are 3 main cosmetic product types:

  • Oil Based
  • Water Based
  • Emulsions

Oil Based

Oil based products are also known as Anhydrous, which is Greek for "without water". These products contain only oil-soluble ingredients. As they have no water, they do not need preservatives to keep them safe and stable. The shelf of these products all depends on the expiry date of ingredients used in the products. These products can take the form of balms, salves, ointments, body oils and body butters.

Some examples of oil soluble ingredients are:

  • Plant oils
  • Waxes
  • Butters
  • Essential oils
  • Vitamin E (Antioxidant)

Water Based

Water based products are also known as Hydrous, which is Greek for "with water”. As these type of products contain water they MUST contain a preservative to delay microorganism growth . If the product does not contain preservatives it will last 5-7 days in fridge. After this period of time it must be discarded. These products can take the form of face toner, body spray, shower gels, shampoos, facial wash and micellar water.

Some examples of water soluble ingredients are:

  • Floral water
  • Herbal infusions
  • Glycerol (plant extracts in glycerine)
  • Gums (thickeners)
  • Preservatives

Emulsions

Emulsions are between oil based and water based products. They contain both oil and water. Emulsions must contain a product called “emulsifying agent “ that normally comes in form of wax to allow water and oil mix. Emulsifying agent normally contain 2 emulsifier ingredients or more. They can be natural, synthetic or both. As emulsions contain water, just like water based products they Must have preservatives.

Emulsions must have an antioxidant (Vitamin E or Rosemary CO2 extract or /and Ascorbyl Palmitate) to protect the oils in the formula from going rancid.

Emulsions must also contain 2 thickeners/stabilizers in their formula. To help thickening and holding the emulsion for long period to avoid separation.
