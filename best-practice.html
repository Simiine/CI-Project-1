Before you begin

Maintain a high level of personal hygiene when product making. Wash your hands and nails and/or use gloves. Tie your hair up and/or wear a net. Ensure all work surfaces are clean, sanitized and dry

You don't need alot of equipment to make skincare products at home. Alot of the equipment can be found both in store and online from basic kitchen equipment to intermediate level cosmetic making tools. Some basic equipment: digital scale (min 0.1g ideally), thermometer, electric hand whisk, heatproof glass jars, pH strips, hand whisk, spoons, and spatulas

Essential Oil Safety

Essential oils should never be applied directly to the skin, as they are highly concentrated and effective in small amounts. They should always be used diluted in plant oils (carriel oil). Safe percentages of use are from 0.3% to 0.5% depending of what type of product you are making, for what type of skin, and for what person.

Packaging & Storage

Store skincare products preferably in glass (dark color) or aluminium containers especially when the products contain essential oils. Essential oils can be corrosive and react with plastic, particularly citrus oils.

Products must be kept dry away from water at ALL TIMES, during the making , handling, use and storage. This is because water is the first cause of microorganism (bacteria, mold, yeast).

Keep your products away from heat , sunlight and handle with care by putting the lid properly every time after use. Temperature, light and oxygen are natural elements which can alter a natural skincare product stability.

Ultimately the best type of packaging is bottles with pump to protect the product from oxidation and avoid putting hands inside the product.

Product Quality

It is important to purchase your ingredients from reliable professional suppliers. Always buy organic and cold pressed oils when possible.

Read INCI (International Nomenclature of Cosmetics Ingredients). This is a universal, worldwide system of naming for cosmetic ingredients. A lot of ingredients have many different common names and using INCI allows you to be 100% sure of the ingredient you are using and how to use it correctly in your formula.
